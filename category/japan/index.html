In anapanasati, book, buddhism, China, consciousness, eastern, health, india, japan, peace, thich nhat hanh, yoga

Baraka (1992)

By anautonomousagent

On January 2, 2013

In art, baraka, China, christian, consciousness, documentary, eastern, history, india, japan, jew, movie, pictures, religion, video, website, western

Great “movie” about human life and religion. I say “movie” because Baraka has “no plot, no dialogue, no actors.”  It contains numerous religious ceremonies and peoples which I never knew existed. The visuals are stunning and I would suggest watching the Blu-Ray version. Really surprising that it only earned $1.3 million in the box office.

Underworld: The Mysterious Origins of Civilizations – Graham Hancock

By anautonomousagent

On January 1, 2013

In book, Egypt, graham hancock, history, japan, mystery, myth, origins


In Underworld: The Mysterious Origins of Civilization, Hancock raises an excellent point — sea levels in ancient times were mush lower than they are now. Thus, many ruins may lie under the water a theme which he fully explores in this novel. The novel is a bit lengthy.

By anautonomousagent

On January 1, 2013

In alan watts, ancient, book, buddhism, China, civilization, consciousness, cosmic, eastern, history, japan, peace, philosophy, universe, zen

The Way of Zen, by Alan Watts, introduces the western mind to the ideas of Zen. The book does a great job, almost as good as any Zen master. Highly recommended for anyone interested in Zen and its history.
